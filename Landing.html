Welcome to Weather Watch



We set out to prove if temperatures increase closer to the equator. On this site you will find various Plots comparing the same group of cities on the same day at the same time to various weather factors that influence the heat index to see if a pattern emerged. When the data was sampled it was winter in the northern hemisphere and summer in the southern hemisphere. (More data at different times of the year across several years would be useful to see if different patterns emerge.)

The heat index, also known as the apparent temperature, is what the temperature feels like to the human body when relative humidity is combined with the air temperature. We also factored in cloudiness and wind speed to test if they contributed to what the temperature feels like to humans.

The plot to the right is a representation of all cities sampled for this project with the color of their marker corresponding with the temperature, with green being cold, moving to yellow and then into red for hot. This graph does not represent the other heat index factors.
